Bismillah

بِسْمِ اللهِ


Meaning: 
‘In the name of Allah’ 
(Book: Bukhari 5376, 5378) 


If one forgets to mention ‘bismillah’ before eating, he should recite the following dua, 



Bismillahi fee avvalihi wa a’kirihi
بِسْمِ اللهِ فِيْ أَوَّلِهِ وَآخِرِهِ


Meaning:
In the name of Allah at the beginning and at the end of it 
(Book: Tirmidhi 1781) 


After eating or drinking: 

Al hamdulillahi katheeran thaiyiban mubarakan feehi ghaira makfiyyin wala muwatha’in wala mustha’a nan anhu rabbana. 


اَلْحَمْدُ للهِ كَثِيرًا طَيِّبًا مُبَارَكًا فِيْهِ غَيْرَ مَكْفِيّ وَلاَ مُوَدَّعٍ وَلاَ مُسْتَغْنًى عَنْهُ رَبَّنَ 

Meaning:
Praise be to Allah Who has satisfied our needs and quenched our thirst. O our Lord! Your favor cannot be compensated, nor can be left, nor can be dispensed with, O our Lord! 
(Book: Bukhari 5458) 


OR 

Alhamdhu lilla hilldhzee kafana wa arwana gaira makfiyyin wala makfoor
اَلْحَمْدُ للهِ الَّذِيْ كَفَانَا وَأَرْوَانَا غَيْرَ مَكْفِيٍّ وَلاَ مَكْفُورٍ


Meaning:
Praise be to Allah Who has satisfied our needs and quenched our thirst. Your favor cannot be compensated or denied. 
(Book: Bukhari 5459) 


OR 

‘Alhamdu lillah’
اَلْحَمْدُ للهِ الَّذِيْ كَفَانَا وَأَرْوَانَا غَيْرَ مَكْفِيٍّ وَلاَ مَكْفُورٍ

Meaning:
Praise be to Allah. 
(Book: Muslim 4915)
