Allahumma innee as’aluka khaira haa wakaira maa feeha wakhaira maa ursilath bihee wa’avoozubika min sharri haa washarri maa feeha washarri maa ursilath bihi 
اَللّهُمَّ إِنِّي أَسْأَلُكَ خَيْرَهَا وَخَيْرَ مَا فِيهَا وَخَيْرَ مَا أُرْسِلَتْ بِهِ وَأَعُوذُ بِكَ مِنْ شَرِّهَا وَشَرِّ مَا فِيهَا وَشَرِّ مَا أُرْسِلَتْ بِهِ

Meaning: 
O Allah! I ask Thee for what is good in it, and the good which it contains, and the good of that which it was sent for. I seek refuge with Thee from what is evil in it, what evil it contains, and the evil of that what it was sent for.
(Book: Muslim 1496)
